The problem

Fund requests used to live in inboxes.

Mid-size organizations approve spend across email threads, spreadsheets, and verbal sign-offs — with no single record of who approved what, when, or why. Deductions like GST and TDS get calculated by hand, and finance teams reconcile everything after the fact.

FlowVault replaces that with one pipeline: a requester submits, a validator checks completeness, a department head approves and sets terms, finance applies the org's own deduction rules, and funds are released — every step timestamped and unchangeable.

The approval chain

Five stages. One rupee at a time.

Each request moves through a fixed sequence — no stage can be skipped, and every stamp is logged to the audit trail.

01
Requester

Submit

A requester drafts a fund request with category, amount, and justification, then submits it into the queue.

02
Validator

Validate

The request is checked for completeness — missing fields or documentation are bounced back before it reaches a department head.

03
Department head

Approve

The department head sets payment terms and approves or rejects the request against their budget.

04
Finance officer

Compute

Finance applies the org's active rules — GST, TDS, policy caps — and confirms the payment schedule.

✓
System

Release

Funds are disbursed and the full chain — request, validation, approval, deductions — is sealed into the audit log.
