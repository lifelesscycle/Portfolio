Say something.
It starts answering before it stops listening.

Aitie is a full streaming voice pipeline — speech in, reasoning, speech out — built so the pause between "you" and "it" disappears. No push-to-talk. No spinner. No waiting for a full sentence to generate before the reply starts speaking.

Why this exists

Latency is what makes AI feel like a machine

Most voice assistants wait for a full transcript, wait for a full answer, then wait for a full audio file before you hear a word. Every one of those waits breaks the illusion of talking to something.

01

Turn-based voice bots feel like walkie-talkies. You speak, there's silence, then a reply arrives all at once. Aitie streams every stage instead, so audio starts while the model is still forming the rest of the sentence.

02

Swapping models usually means rewriting the app. Aitie keeps ASR, reasoning, and TTS as interchangeable modules behind one interface, so a faster model can drop in without touching the pipeline around it.

Architecture

One continuous stream, not three separate steps

Audio, text, and reasoning tokens all flow forward concurrently — the moment the first few words of a reply exist, synthesis begins on them while the model keeps generating the rest.
